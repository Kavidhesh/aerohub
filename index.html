Podcast Summary: CFM Leap Engine Ramp-Up, Durability, and Spares in 2025

In this podcast, Aviation Week editors discussed key developments regarding the CFM Leap engine program, focusing on durability improvements, the ramp-up of production, and aftermarket services.

Source: Aviation Week

Durability Issues and Solutions:

  • CFM Leap engines (1A & 1B) faced coating and bearing upgrade issues.
  • Introduced solutions include:
    • Improved coatings
    • Reverse bleed systems to reduce coking
    • New turbine blades for harsher conditions

Production and Ramp-Up:

  • Over 3,500 Leap-powered aircraft in service, fleet expected to double by 2030.
  • Production increase of 15%-20% this year.
  • Significant investments in aftermarket network and new maintenance facilities.

Aftermarket and Spares:

  • High demand for spare engines, resulting in longer lead times and higher leasing rates.
  • Spare engine rates doubled from previous generations.
  • Production challenges expected to improve by 2025.

Market Growth and Future Outlook:

  • LEAP program is maturing, with third-party MRO providers gaining more market share.
  • MRO activities and shop visits expected to increase as fleet grows.

Indian Aircraft MRO Industry Set for Significant Growth by FY26

India's aircraft maintenance, repair, and overhaul (MRO) sector is poised for substantial expansion, with revenue projected to reach Rs 4,500 crore in fiscal year 2026—a 50% increase from fiscal 2024.

This growth is primarily driven by the expanding fleet size of Indian airlines and recent GST rationalizations.

Key Factors

  • Fleet Expansion: Domestic operators' fleets are expected to grow by 20-25% by next year
  • GST Reduction: GST on aircraft components and services lowered to 5%, improving Indian MROs' competitiveness.
  • Service Diversification: Indian MROs are expanding offerings, potentially raising market penetration to 20% by next fiscal

Financial Outlook

  • Profitability expected to improve to ~20% by fiscal 2026.
  • Working capital cycle projected to improve by 20-25 days.
  • Interest coverage ratio to rise to 3-3.3 times in next fiscal, up from 2.7 times in FY24

Challenges remain in ramping up hangar capacities, developing a local ecosystem for aviation spare parts, and training skilled manpower Despite these hurdles, the industry's growth trajectory and improving financial metrics signal a strengthening of credit profiles for MRO players in the medium term

MRO Industry Expands Potential AI Use Cases

This article highlights the growing applications of artificial intelligence (AI) in the MRO sector of aviation.

Source: Aviation Week

Key Takeaways

  • AI applications are expanding beyond predictive maintenance to simplify maintenance processes themselves
  • GE Aerospace has developed AI-powered tools for engine inspections, improving speed, accuracy, and consistency
  • Aiir Innovations offers an AI tool for automated aeroengine borescope inspections, analyzing video 75% faster than manual reviews
  • Generative AI is being used to:
    • Simplify access to maintenance records (GE Aerospace, Accenture, and Microsoft's GenAI Assistant)
    • Provide technician assistance (AFI KLM E&M, Amygda, LexX Technologies, and Veryon)
    • Improve parts traceability and authenticity (GA Telesis + Alitheon's FeaturePrint)
  • AFI KLM E&M has launched over 80 generative AI projects, including tools like Charlie (for parts/documentation searches) and Voice to Admin (natural language processing)
  • AI is also being applied to enhance customer service, recruitment, and sustainability efforts

Conclusion

These advancements demonstrate the MRO sector's commitment to leveraging AI for improved efficiency, accuracy, and safety in aircraft maintenance

DeepSeek AI and Sixth-Gen Jets: China's Technological Leap Forward

China has made significant strides in artificial intelligence and military technology, challenging Western dominance in innovation and defense. Two recent developments highlight this progress.

1. DeepSeek AI: A Cost-Effective Breakthrough

DeepSeek, a Chinese AI chatbot, has disrupted the AI industry with its impressive performance and cost-efficiency:

  • Developed with just $6 million, compared to billions invested by Western competitors
  • Rivals top AI models in performance
  • Became the most downloaded app on Apple's US App Store
  • Subscription cost of $0.50 per month, versus ChatGPT's $20

The impact has been significant:

  • Global AI and semiconductor stocks plummeted
  • Nvidia lost nearly $600 billion in market value in one day

2. Sixth-Generation Fighter Jets: Redefining Aerial Warfare

China unveiled two sixth-generation stealth fighter jets in December 2024, featuring:

  • Advanced AI-driven automation
  • Extended range
  • Greater payload capacities

This puts China years ahead of competitors, with the US not expecting operational sixth-generation fighters until the 2030s.

Global Reactions and Implications

  • US President Donald Trump called it a "wake-up call," pledging $500 billion to the 'Stargate' AI initiative
  • China's R&D spending reached 3.6 trillion yuan (~$496 billion) in 2024, up 8.3% from previous year
  • R&D investment intensity in China has grown steadily, reaching 2.58% in 2023

These advancements have reignited the global arms race and tech innovation sprint, potentially reshaping competitive dynamics and global power structures.
